1 The Problem

Banko Chow was managing food orders the way most small businesses do — customers sending WhatsApp messages, a human reading them, manually confirming, replying, and then updating a spreadsheet by hand. As order volume grew, this became a bottleneck. Orders were missed, replies were slow, and inventory tracking was always a step behind.

The business needed a system that could handle orders 24/7 without a human in the loop for routine confirmations — while still keeping the owner in control of approvals.

2 How It Works

The workflow handles the entire order journey automatically — from a customer's first WhatsApp message to inventory being updated in Google Sheets.

Workflow Flow

Customer WhatsApp Message → Dedup Check → Filter Valid Messages → AI Order Agent (GPT-4o Mini) → Parse Order → Order Confirmed? → Email Approval to Owner → Approve / Reject → WA Confirmation → Update Inventory Sheet

Deduplication ensures the same message doesn't trigger duplicate orders. The AI agent understands natural language orders ("I want 2 jollof and a drink") and structures them into a confirmed order object. The owner receives an email with approve/reject links — one click confirms or cancels. Once approved, the customer gets a WhatsApp confirmation and inventory is automatically decremented in Google Sheets.
